  1. Ready to burn some turtles?

    Before you begin your kanji learning adventure, will you join the Crabigator on a quick tour of the website?

  2. Lessons and Reviews

    Learning Japanese radicals, kanji and vocabulary are broken into two sections: Lessons and Reviews

    The number of items available for you to learn and review are displayed in the navigation.

  3. Lessons

    New radicals, kanji, and vocabulary are introduced to you in lessons. After going through a few lessons, you will be quizzed on the material. We employ the use of mnemonics to help you recall information.

    Once you passed the quizzing, the kotoba will be added to the Review queue.

    New lessons appear after certain requirements are met during your reviews.

  4. Reviews

    Reviews are the backbone of your radical, kanji, and vocabulary learning. Here you will be quizzed on the meaning and readings for kotoba that you have done lessons on.

    To maximize your ability to recall the meanings and readings, WaniKani uses a spaced repetition system (SRS) to help maximize your retention of the item.

  5. Spaced Repetition System

    SRS

    Everytime you answer both the meaning and reading of the item correctly, it will move up an SRS level. Each increase in SRS level results in a larger time interval between your previous review and the reapperance of the kotoba in the reviews queue.

    Depending on how many times you have answered the kotoba wrong, the kotoba can drop numerous SRS levels

    We use a nine-level SRS that are grouped into five parts.

  6. SRS Apprentice

    The first few SRS levels are grouped into Apprentice. Kotoba under Apprentice are either typically new to you or items you may have trouble with.

    Time intervals for each level range from a couple hours to a few days.

  7. SRS Guru

    Guru represents items that you are getting a hang of. It consists of a couple SRS levels.

    Time intervals for each level range from a week to a couple weeks.

  8. SRS Master

    Mastered items are just what they sound like; you have begun getting a good grasp at recognizing the item's meaning and reading!

    Time interval for this level is a month.

  9. SRS Enlightened

    Items at this level has transcended time and space. You don't need to think what the meaning and readings are, it comes naturally.

    Time interval for this level is a few months.

  10. SRS Burned

    In your enlightened state, you decided to share your knowledge to the mortals by burning the kanji into turtle shells. You have proven your ability to recall the item's meaning and reading.

    Items in this level are removed from the review queue. They can be resurrected by accessing their individual pages under the Levels, Radicals, Kanji, or Vocabulary dropdowns located on the navigation bar.

  11. Radicals, Kanji, and Vocabulary

    ことば

    What are radicals, kanji, and vocabulary exactly?

  12. Radicals

    Radicals are the building blocks of kanji. Most kanji contain radical components that can be found in other kanji. The color blue is used to identify radicals.

    We employ the use of mnemonics in our teachings that utilize these radicals to create a story in order to recall meaning and readings of kanji.

  13. Kanji

    Kanji are the Chinese characters used in the Japanese writing system. They are the building blocks for vocabulary. Kanji have two groups of readings: On'yomi (Chinese reading) and Kun'yomi. Within these groups kanji may contain many readings or none at all.

    Instead of forcing you to learn both groups of readings, for Kanji we only ask for a single reading from the most common reading group, as we feel this is most important. The less common reading group may be covered later on if it appears in vocabulary.

    Pink is used to identify kanji.

  14. Vocabulary

    Vocabulary are the combination of kanji and kana to form words used in speech and writing

    Purple is used to identify vocabulary.

    There is one last thing we would like to cover before the end of the tour: the leveling system.

  15. Leveling System

    +1

    New material are presented to you in levels. Each level contains a number of radicals, kanji, and vocabulary.

    When reaching a new level, all radicals of that level are automatically unlocked to you for lessons.

    Kanji can only be unlocked if the radicals that compose it have reached SRS Guru and if your current level is equal to or greater than the kanji level.

    Vocabulary unlocks occur when every kanji used to compose the word have reached Guru and if your current level is equal to or greater than the vocab's level.

  16. How To Level Up?

    90%

    Leveling up is largely dictated by the percentage of current level kanji that have made it to Guru, which is set to 90%.

    Radicals have an indirect effect to leveling up since kanji unlocks rely on your radical performance.

    Your performance in vocabulary has no effect on how you level.
